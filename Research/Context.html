How Perception Affects Art:


One of the main informing ideas behind my research was the idea of how perspective, or the way in which art is observed, affects the art itself. I draw this idea from artists such Marcel Duchamp and Andy Warhol, with pieces such as Fountain or the Campbell Soup Can works, or the concepts behind suprematism and premade sculpture. Essentially, the meaning of art is entirely relative, and perspective not only affects the meaning of art, but creates and defines it. The article ‘From perception to art: how vision creates meanings’, written by Baingio Pinna and Adam Reeves, which argues that “perceptual meanings are ultimately related to the highest conceptual artistic products”, was the most influential article I read on this specific topic
