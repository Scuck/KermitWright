The argument present in this website revolves around artists such as Marcel Duchamp, Andy Worhol, and similar artists who use their art to ask questions about art itself, through the use of different interpretations across the audience to create simultaneous answers to questions that cannot be answered with a single answer. This assertion that the simultaneous differences in interpretation of a piece of art across different observers represent real, meaningful, and valid interpretations of that artwork originates in ‘From perception to art: how vision creates meanings’, written by Baingio Pinna and Adam Reeves, where they argue that “perceptual meanings are ultimately related to the highest conceptual artistic products” I also use the concepts of suprematism, premade sculptures, and other such art movements, which claim that art can essentially be anything, if it is perceived to be art, can be art as a tool to analyze the power of perception for art.
